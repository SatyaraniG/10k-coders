AP Train Accident: Both passenger trains lacked anti-collision system Kavach

Synopsis: The East Coast Railway has called for a thorough investigation to determine the cause of the train collision in Vizianagaram. The incident resulted in the tragic loss of 13 lives and left over 50 people injured. Both trains were not equipped with the railways' safety system, Kavach. The loco pilot and assistant loco pilot of the Rayagada Passenger train were among the casualties.

The East Coast Railway has stated that a thorough investigation is required to ascertain the factors that led to the collision of the Palasa Passenger train with the Rayagada Passenger train in Kantakapalli, Vizianagaram district. The incident resulted in the tragic loss of 13 lives and left over 50 people injured. As per a TOI report, notably, both trains were not equipped with the railways' safety system, Kavach. Saurabh Prasad, the divisional railway manager of Waltair division, revealed that the Palasa Passenger train was moving on the middle line and had slowed down to a speed of 10 to 15 kmph. Meanwhile, the Rayagada Passenger train overshot the red signal and collided with the Palasa Passenger train from behind. Prasad added that it is too early to speculate on human error or signal failure as the accident requires a comprehensive inquiry.
